How to use the visualizer

The setup mirrors the class bridge project: a 6-inch span between a pin support (left, ▲) and a roller (right, ○). The Tester load case models the Vernier tester's plate pulling straight down at the center of your deck, and predicts the load at which your first member fails.

Build: tap the sheet to add joints (they snap to the ½-inch grid). Switch to the Member tool and tap two joints to connect them. The bottom row of joints between the supports is your deck.

Read it: blue members are in tension (being stretched), red members are in compression (being squeezed). Thicker lines are closer to their limit. A member past its limit gets an ✕.

  • A solvable truss follows 2 × joints = members + 3. Too few members → mechanism (collapses). Too many → indeterminate (can't be solved by the method of joints).
  • The Inspect tool shows the free-body diagram at any joint — the same picture you draw for method-of-joints calculations. The vector sum is zero: that's equilibrium.
  • Forces here are ideal 2D truss values: members only carry axial load, joints are pins. Your real bridge has glue joints, member bending, and imperfections — expect real failure loads to differ. The pattern of which members work hardest is the insight.

Moving load: steps a point load across the deck joints so you can watch member forces shift as a load crosses — some members even flip between tension and compression.
